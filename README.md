# Creative Writing Masterclass

A collection of lessons and guides exploring the craft of creative writing — from narrative structures that span cultures to poetry traditions from around the globe.

## Lessons

- **[Storytelling Across Cultures](lessons/storytelling-across-cultures.md)** — How narrative structures differ around the world: the Western three-act structure, Japanese kishotenketsu, Arabic oral tradition, and Latin American magical realism. Understanding these frameworks makes you a more versatile and empathetic writer.

- **[Poetry Forms Around the World](lessons/poetry-forms-around-the-world.md)** — A tour of global poetry traditions including haiku, ghazal, sonnet, limerick, tanka, and free verse. Each form carries the cultural DNA of the people who created it.

- **[Reading Like a Writer](lessons/reading-like-a-writer.md)** — Practical techniques for analyzing published literature to sharpen your own craft. Learn to reverse-engineer what makes great writing work.

## Who This Is For

- Aspiring fiction writers looking to develop their craft
- Students of literature who want to write, not just analyze
- Anyone curious about how stories and poems work across different cultures
- Writers who feel stuck and want fresh frameworks for thinking about narrative

## Philosophy

Great writing doesn't come from following a single formula. It comes from understanding many approaches, absorbing diverse traditions, and finding the techniques that resonate with your own voice. These lessons are designed to broaden your creative toolkit, not narrow it.
